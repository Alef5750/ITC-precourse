A Bit About Me

Why Coding?

There are several reasons why I've begun to learn coding. My interest initially set in when a mentor of mine recommended I look into it. After chatting with some friends and family members who have years of experience learning and teaching coding, I decided to check it out for myself. There were, and are, several things about coding which I am enjoying (so far;).
Firstly, there's the element of personal creativity: when coding HTML and CSS, there's a positive sensation I feel when I can look at a webpage and say: "Wow, I created that."
Secondly, there's the element of learning a new skill: I have always enjoyed learning new things, and I am particularly enjoying learning a new skill, (as opposed to information further removed from practical application, as has been much of my learning in educational systems, for better or for worse).
Thirdly, there's the problem-solving side of things: challenge is exciting to me, and I'm enjoying having to work hard to figure things out, and apply newly acquired knowledge as I learn more and more.

Education and Career

After finishing high-school at Yeshivat Rambam (in my hometown of Baltimore, Maryland), I moved to Israel, where I studied in a Yeshiva – Yeshivat HaKotel – in Jerusalem. Over the course of my 9 years there, I completed a degree in Education from Lifshitz Teacher's College, specializing in Tanach and Torah Shebaal Peh. Teaching is something I have always enjoyed, and it was a natural decision to pursue a B.Ed.
After having taught English-speaking students informally in Yeshivat HaKotel for five-or-so years, I decided to take my teaching interest to the Israeli classroom. Due to the high demand for English teachers, my first job was teaching English to beginner and intermediate level students at Or Torah Stone, a middle-school in Jerusalem. After my first year of teaching in the classroom, it became clear that this was not the path I had envisioned, nor was it one I intended on continuing to pursue.
The following year, newly married, I began working for Machon HaTorah VeHaaretz, a company in the south of Israel that prepares and promotes various educational materials relating to mitzvot hateluyot baaretz (commandments relating to the Land of Israel). I was responsible for translating content into English, as well as a member of the fundraising team. After several months I was feeling that I wasn't maximizing my capabilities, and searched for a new job, which I found shortly thereafter, and where I would work for the next two years, until the Corona virus came into the picture. At this new job, I worked as the main customer service agent at Koren Publishers Jerusalem, a Jewish-content publishing company, famous for being the first-ever Jewish company to publish the Tanach (Bible). (Before Koren Publishers, the Bible had only been published by Christians.) Koren Publishers was a fantastic place to work, with splendid coworkers, but professionally, I felt I was underchallenged, and capable of much more. When the Corona virus hit, Koren was forced to fire nearly a quarter of their employees. As sad as they were to have to let me go, I grabbed the opportunity to explore new areas. This is when I heard about coding, and here we are!

Personal Interests

I enjoy sports, and have every since I could walk and hold a ball. I often play basketball, although I wish I could find more opportunities for tennis too. I try to find time to meditate, and do yoga, and I've kept a written journal for the past ten-plus years. I particularly enjoy journaling, as it helps me clear my mind, make better decisions, and feel less stressed I enjoy hiking, and find special pleasure in the desert in Israel's south (which conjures memories of my basic-training days in the Israeli Army).

Places I've Lived

I was born in Palo Alto, California, in Stanford University Hospital, but three months later, my parents moved us to Baltimore, Maryland, where I spent my entire childhood, until moving to Israel after high-school. After 9 years in Jerusalem, I got married to my wonderful wife Esther, and we currently live in Elazar, a small town in Gush Etzion, about half-an-hour south of Jerusalem. In total, I've lived in four cities in my life:

  1. Palo Alto, California
  2. Baltimore, Maryland
  3. Jerusalem, Israel
  4. Elazar, Israel
